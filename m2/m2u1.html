Unit 1 task:

Injection poses a risk to computer security as outlined by OWASP who ranked injection as the 3rd most important risk to web applications. (OWASP, 2021). Injection attacks can lead to the theft of many assets from an association such as sensitive data. (UC Berkley, 2024). Injection attacks pose a particular threat when using shared networks as injections can be used to not only steal information but also to install malicious software. (GuardRails, 2023). To help in the prevention of injection attacks networks and data must be secured. As outlined by Kime (2023) there are 5 keyways to help prevent injection attacks. These are: filter database inputs, restrict database code, restrict database access, maintain applications and databases, monitor application and database inputs and communications. (Kime, 2023). Each of these approaches outlined by Kime assist in reduction the chance of an injection attack by limiting the inputs that can be made by a user and ensuring that technology is maintained to prevent vulnerabilities. The use of injection attacks has become 5 times more common than presentation attacks due to the use of AI and deepfakes (Painter, 2024). This shows the relevance of injection attacks as a threat to modern cyber security.
